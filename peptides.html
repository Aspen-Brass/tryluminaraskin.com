Ingredient Spotlight

Peptides

Multi-Peptide Complexes

Peptides are the building blocks of proteins in your skin. In cosmetic formulations, they help support the appearance of firmer, smoother, more youthful-looking skin.

What Are Peptides?

Peptides are short chains of amino acids - the same building blocks that make up proteins like collagen and elastin in your skin. In skincare, peptides are used to support the skin's natural appearance and help address visible signs of aging.

Different peptides have different cosmetic functions. Some help with the appearance of firmness, others target the look of fine lines, and some support overall skin texture. That's why many effective skincare products use multiple peptides together.

Types of Peptides in Skincare

Signal Peptides

Help support the skin's natural processes that contribute to a firmer, more youthful appearance. GHK-Cu is an example of a signal peptide.

Neurotransmitter-Inhibiting Peptides

Help relax the appearance of facial muscles to soften the look of expression lines. Syn-Ake is an example of this type of peptide.

Carrier Peptides

Help deliver trace elements like copper to the skin, supporting overall skin appearance and health. Copper peptides fall into this category.

Enzyme-Inhibiting Peptides

Help protect the skin's natural proteins from breakdown, supporting a more youthful, resilient appearance over time.

Cosmetic Benefits

When used in skincare formulations, peptides may help:

  • Support the appearance of firmer, more resilient skin
  • Improve the look of fine lines and wrinkles
  • Enhance skin texture and smoothness
  • Promote a more youthful, radiant appearance
  • Support the skin's natural barrier function

Note: These are cosmetic benefits related to appearance. Peptides are not medical treatments.
